# English Curriculum (Micro-Learning Approach)

This curriculum covers the entire spectrum of English proficiency from A1 (Beginner) to C2 (Mastery). Designed specifically for Brazilian Portuguese speakers, the structure follows the app's micro-learning approach (5–10 minute lessons) and strongly emphasizes **conversational English, phrasal verbs, collocations, and idioms** to ensure learners sound natural, not just grammatically correct.

## Module A1: Beginner (Lessons 1–30)
1. Subject Pronouns (*I, you, he, she, it, we, they*)
2. The verb *To Be* in the present (Affirmative)
3. The verb *To Be* in the present (Negative and Interrogative)
4. Definite and Indefinite Articles (*A, An, The*)
5. Demonstrative Pronouns (*This, That, These, Those*)
6. Plural Nouns (Regular *-s* and basic irregulars like *men, women*)
7. Possessive Adjectives (*My, your, his, her, its, our, their*)
8. The Possessive 'S (*John's car*) vs. *Of* (*The door of the car*)
9. Simple Present: *I, You, We, They* (Habits and routines)
10. Simple Present: *He, She, It* (Adding *-s* / *-es*)
11. Negative and Questions in Simple Present (*Do / Does*)
12. Adverbs of Frequency (*Always, usually, sometimes, never*)
13. Question Words: *What, Who, Where*
14. Question Words: *When, Why, How*
15. *There is / There are* (Expressing existence)
16. Prepositions of Place: *In, On, At*
17. Prepositions of Time: *In, On, At*
18. Modal Verb: *Can* / *Can't* (Ability and Permission)
19. Present Continuous (*Am/Is/Are* + *-ing*) - Actions happening now
20. Present Continuous vs. Simple Present
21. Telling the Time (*It's half past two*)
22. Daily Routine Vocabulary and Collocations (*Take a shower, have breakfast*)
23. The Verb *To Have* (Possession and Meals: *have a car, have lunch*)
24. Common Adjectives and their Opposites (*Big/small, hot/cold*)
25. Subject vs. Object Pronouns (*Me, him, her, us, them*)
26. Countable Nouns and Plurals
27. Uncountable Nouns (*Water, information, bread*)
28. Quantifiers: *Some* vs. *Any*
29. Quantifiers: *How much* vs. *How many*
30. Basic Phrasal Verbs for beginners (*Wake up, stand up, sit down*)

39. Future plans with *Going To*
40. Future predictions and promises with *Will*

42. Modal Verb: *Should / Shouldn't* (Giving advice)
43. Expressing Obligation: *Have to* vs. *Must*
44. Expressing Lack of Obligation: *Don't have to*
45. Modal Verb: *Would like to* (Polite requests and offers)
46. Pronouns: *Something, Anything, Nothing, Everything*
47. Pronouns: *Someone, Anyone, No one, Everyone*

49. Adverbs of Manner (Adding *-ly*: *slowly, quickly*)

54. Expressions of quantity: *A lot of, many, much, a few, a little*
55. Exclamations: *What a...!* and *How...!*
56. The weather and seasons

77. Relative Pronouns: *Who, Which, That* (Defining relative clauses)

79. Non-defining relative clauses (Adding extra information)
80. Passive Voice: Present Simple (*Is made, are sold*)
81. Passive Voice: Past Simple (*Was built, were written*)
82. *So... that* and *Such... that* (Cause and effect)
83. *Both, Either, Neither*
84. Causative Form Introduction (*Have / Get something done*)
85. Phrasal Verbs with *Get* (Part 1: *Get up, get back, get over*)
86. Phrasal Verbs with *Take* (*Take off, take up, take out*)

89. Conversational Idioms: Money (*Costs an arm and a leg, go broke*)

91. Agreeing and disagreeing naturally (*I couldn't agree more, I don't think so*)

114. Future Perfect (*Will have done*)

133. Hedging: Softening your speech (*It seems that, arguably, presumably, to some extent*)
134. Phrasal verb nouns and adjectives (*A breakthrough, a letdown, worn-out*)

136. Advanced Phrasal Verbs for Business & Negotiation (*Draw up, back down, fall through*)
137. Phrasal verbs with multiple complex meanings (*Set up, work out, make out*)
138. Nuanced Linking words (*Nevertheless, furthermore, conversely, albeit*)
139. Advanced Collocations with prepositions (*Comply with, derive from, devoid of*)
140. Idioms: Dealing with difficult situations (*Bite the bullet, face the music*)
